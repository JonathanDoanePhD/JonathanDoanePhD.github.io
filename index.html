Count spoken words

A child can count words in speach; why do computers struggle? Standard methodology looks for brief pauses and dips in volume between words for counting; therein lies the problem. Everything from pitch to verbal ticks depend on the individual!

To solve this problem, I developed an audio analyzer that counts words in speach, twice as accurate as the standard.
This was achieved through multiple linear regression on both pause and silence thresholds against vocal qualities.
I completed this project within one week, at the Erdős Institute.
